Website Evaluations

Apple

The Apple website is sleek, professional, and visually consistent. It clearly communicates its brand identity and makes navigation simple for users.

  • Contrast: Apple uses strong contrast between white backgrounds and bold product images, making products stand out.
  • Repetition: Consistent fonts, colors, and layout reinforce the brand identity across every page.
  • Alignment: Everything is cleanly aligned with grids, which makes the site easy to scan and visually balanced.
  • Proximity: Related elements, like product details and pricing, are grouped together, making it easy to find relevant information.

Ling's Cars

Ling’s Cars is a well-known example of bad design. It’s overloaded with animations, clashing colors, and distracting content, making it overwhelming to users.

  • Contrast: Overuse of bright colors, flashing elements, and poor text/background contrast makes it painful to look at.
  • Repetition: Instead of consistency, there are too many competing design styles, fonts, and graphics with no unity.
  • Alignment: The page feels chaotic because text, images, and widgets are not aligned in any logical grid or pattern.
  • Proximity: Information is scattered and unrelated elements are crammed together, making it hard to focus on what matters.
